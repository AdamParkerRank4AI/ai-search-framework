## ROUND 1: PREMIER LEAGUE RECORDS & STATS
*10 questions - 1 point each unless stated*

**Q1.** Who has made the most Premier League appearances of all time?
> **Answer:** James Milner (655 appearances) - *Bonus point for exact number. He overtook Gareth Barry's record of 653.*

**Q2.** Who holds the record for the most Premier League clean sheets by a goalkeeper?
> **Answer:** Petr Cech (202 clean sheets) - *Bonus point for exact number*

**Q3.** Who scored the fastest hat-trick in Premier League history? Bonus point for the time.
> **Answer:** Sadio Mane - 2 minutes 56 seconds (Southampton vs Aston Villa, 16 May 2015)

**Q4.** Which player has scored the most own goals in Premier League history?
> **Answer:** Richard Dunne (10 own goals)

**Q5.** Who holds the record for the most Premier League yellow cards?
> **Answer:** Gareth Barry (123 yellow cards)

**Q6.** Which team holds the record for the longest unbeaten run in Premier League history, and how many games?
> **Answer:** Arsenal - 49 games (May 2003 to October 2004) - *1 point for team, 1 point for number*

**Q7.** Who ended Arsenal's 49-game unbeaten run, and what was the score?
> **Answer:** Manchester United, 2-0 at Old Trafford (24 October 2004) - *1 point for team, 1 point for score*

**Q8.** Who holds the record for scoring in the most consecutive Premier League matches?
> **Answer:** Jamie Vardy - 11 consecutive matches (Leicester City, 2015-16 season)

**Q9.** Who has scored the most Premier League hat-tricks?
> **Answer:** Sergio Aguero (12 hat-tricks, all for Manchester City). Alan Shearer is second with 11.

**Q10.** Who holds the record for the most goals in a single Premier League season (38-game era)?
> **Answer:** Erling Haaland - 36 goals (Manchester City, 2022-23)

## ROUND 2: WORLD FOOTBALL
*8 questions - variable points*

**Q11.** Since 2002, six different countries have won the World Cup. Name them all. (1 point each, 6 available)
> **Answer:** Brazil (2002), Italy (2006), Spain (2010), Germany (2014), France (2018), Argentina (2022)

**Q12.** Who scored the winning goal in the 2010 World Cup final?
> **Answer:** Andres Iniesta (Spain 1-0 Netherlands, extra time)

**Q13.** In the famous 2005 Champions League final, Liverpool came back from 3-0 down against AC Milan. Name all three Liverpool goalscorers. (1 point each)
> **Answer:** Steven Gerrard, Vladimir Smicer, Xabi Alonso

**Q14.** Which manager has won the Champions League / European Cup the most times?
> **Answer:** Carlo Ancelotti (5 times) - AC Milan 2003 & 2007, Real Madrid 2014, 2022 & 2024

**Q15.** Greece famously won Euro 2004. Who scored the winning goal in the final, and who did they beat?
> **Answer:** Angelos Charisteas scored; they beat Portugal 1-0 - *1 point each*

**Q16.** Which was the first Asian country to reach the semi-finals of a World Cup?
> **Answer:** South Korea (2002 World Cup, as co-hosts)

**Q17.** Name the two countries that jointly hosted the 2002 World Cup. (1 point each)
> **Answer:** South Korea and Japan

**Q18.** True or false: No English manager has ever won the Champions League (post-1992 rebrand).
> **Answer:** True - Brian Clough won the European Cup, but no English manager has won the Champions League since it was rebranded in 1992.

## ROUND 3: WHO AM I?
*10 questions - 1 point each*

**Q19.** I'm 6ft 7 inches tall. I'm famous for my robot celebration. I married a famous model. I played for more than 10 professional clubs including QPR, Portsmouth, Aston Villa, Norwich, Southampton, Liverpool, Tottenham, Stoke, and Burnley. Who am I?

**Q26.** I played for Aston Villa, Manchester United, Blackburn Rovers, Birmingham, and Sunderland. I'm from Trinidad and Tobago. I scored in the 1999 Champions League Final. Who am I?

**Q27.** I'm Spanish. I came through Barcelona's academy, joined Arsenal as a teenager where I became captain, went back to Barcelona, then moved to Chelsea. I retired at Como in Italy. Who am I?

## ROUND 5: ODD ONE OUT
*6 questions - 2 points each (1 for the odd one out, 1 for the reason)*

**Q34.** Odd one out: Manchester United, Arsenal, Chelsea, Manchester City, Liverpool, Leicester City?
> **Answer:** **Leicester City** - the only Premier League champion from outside the traditional "Big Six." (Also accept: the only one based in the East Midlands)

**Q35.** Odd one out: Steven Gerrard, Frank Lampard, Paul Scholes, David Beckham?
> **Answer:** **Steven Gerrard** - the only one who never won the Premier League.

**Q36.** Odd one out: Paris Saint-Germain, Real Madrid, Barcelona, Bayern Munich, Manchester City?
> **Answer:** **Manchester City** - the only club to have been relegated to the third tier of their domestic league. (City were in the old Division Two in 1998-99)

**Q37.** Odd one out: Didier Drogba, Thierry Henry, Sergio Aguero, Robin van Persie, Luis Suarez?
> **Answer:** **Luis Suarez** - the only one who never won the Premier League.

**Q38.** Odd one out: Petr Cech, David Seaman, Edwin van der Sar, Peter Schmeichel?
> **Answer:** **David Seaman** - the only one who never won the Champions League.

**Q39.** Odd one out: Ryan Giggs, Paul Scholes, Gary Neville, David Beckham?
> **Answer:** **David Beckham** - the only member of the "Class of '92" who did not finish his career at Manchester United. Giggs, Scholes, and Neville all retired at Man Utd.

## ROUND 6: TRUE OR FALSE
*8 questions - 1 point each*

**Q40.** True or false: Leicester City have won more Premier League titles than Tottenham Hotspur.
> **Answer:** **True** (Leicester 1, Tottenham 0)

**Q41.** True or false: Arsenal went an entire 38-game Premier League season without losing a single match.
> **Answer:** **True** (2003-04, "The Invincibles")

**Q42.** True or false: Petr Cech wore his famous rugby-style headguard because of a skull fracture suffered in a Premier League match against Reading.
> **Answer:** **True** (October 2006, collision with Stephen Hunt's knee)

**Q43.** True or false: Harry Kane won at least one trophy during his time at Tottenham Hotspur.
> **Answer:** **False** (Kane won no trophies at Spurs)

**Q44.** True or false: Erling Haaland scored more Premier League goals in his debut season (2022-23) than Thierry Henry managed in any single season at Arsenal.
> **Answer:** **True** (Haaland scored 36; Henry's best was 30 in 2003-04)

**Q45.** True or false: More teams from London have won the Premier League than teams from the North West of England.
> **Answer:** **False** (London: Arsenal, Chelsea = 2 clubs. North West: Manchester United, Blackburn Rovers, Manchester City, Liverpool = 4 clubs)

**Q46.** True or false: The fastest Premier League hat-trick was scored in under 3 minutes.
> **Answer:** **True** (Sadio Mane, 2 minutes 56 seconds)

**Q47.** True or false: A Premier League goalkeeper has scored a goal directly from a goal kick.
> **Answer:** **True** (Asmir Begovic scored from a wind-assisted clearance for Stoke City vs Southampton in 2013, measured at approximately 91.9 metres)
